Ingredients of Red Sauce Chicken

  1. You need of Main Ingredients.
  2. Prepare 1 pack of chicken breast.
  3. It's to taste of Okra (desired amount).
  4. You need 1 can of corn.
  5. You need 2 cups of Rice.
  6. You need of Oil (preferably olive).
  7. Prepare 1 can of tomato sauce or 1 tube of tomato paste.
  8. It's of Lemon juice (or salt and vinegar).
  9. It's of White wine (preference, not needed).
  10. Prepare of Seasoning.
  11. You need of Ground annatto.
  12. Prepare of Ground ginger.
  13. Prepare of Garlic powder.
  14. It's of Onion powder.
  15. You need of Cilantro.
  16. You need of Parsley.
  17. Prepare of Chives.
  18. It's of Salt.
  19. It's of Chili powder.
  20. You need of Diced garlic.
  21. It's 1/2 of Diced onion.

Red Sauce Chicken instructions

  1. Soak okra in water with lemons juice or vinegar and salt..
  2. Dice the onions and garlic into small cubes. Cut each chicken breast into three pieces..
  3. In a pot, use your preferred oil (we use olive). Pop the chicken in once it's hot. Have the stove at a medium heat temperature..
  4. While the chicken cooks, cook rice. Use rice cooker, or a pot for better results. Add a dash of salt and onion powder..
  5. In a separate pot, use cooking oil. Put the stove on high heat. Drain the okra, and place it into the pot. Once most of the "drool" of the okra is gone, it'll be ready. Add more oil if it takes longer. Add salt once it's almost ready..
  6. Back to the chicken, put it on high heat. Put the onions and garlic into it. Add white wine (preference, not needed). Use parsley, chives, cilantro for greens. Add a dash of ground ginger, onion powder, garlic powder, chili powder. Add plenty of ground annatto. Add salt to preference. When the onions turn golden, that's means the chicken is ready. Put the stove back to low temperature..
  7. Add the tomato sauce (preferably unflavored) or tomato paste. Add water to preference (less water = more creamy). Let it boil so the chicken absorbs the flavors. Add corn (preference). You can add the okra, or keep it separate depending on who's eating..
  8. And it's ready! Enjoy.
